  4. What Happens When I enter personal information Online?
    1. How is the Information that I Send to You Protected? All of the information that you transmit to us electronically, including basic information necessary for basic calculators as well as personally identifiable information disclosed during registration, including credit card and other personally identifiable information is securely protected using at a minimum SSL (Secure Socket Layer) 128-bit encryption. VeriSign provides the digital certificate enabling us to employ SSL encryption. Furthermore, we have implemented strict policy guidelines to safeguard the privacy of your personally identifiable financial information from unauthorized access or improper use, and we will continue to enhance our security procedures as new technology becomes available.
    2. How Do You Ensure the Security of the Payment Information Once I Give it to You? Processing of online software subscriptions is facilitated through Authorize.net. When you submit your credit card information, it is securely transmitted to Authorize.net and a Recurring Billing profile is established. We temporarily receive, but do not permanently store, complete credit card or EFT information on our server or at our offline site. For security and verification purposes we store the last four digits only of an online purchaser’s credit card. The credit card’s last four digits are stored with the purchase order(s) with which they are associated and are stored only for use as record locators and to verify purchase orders.
